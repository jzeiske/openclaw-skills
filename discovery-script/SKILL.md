# Discovery Script

The exact questions to ask on a 15-20 minute discovery call so the prospect walks themselves into admitting they need your solution.

## What This Skill Does

**Input:** A specific industry or niche (and optionally, the specific problem you suspect they have)

**Output:** A structured discovery call script — opener, rapport questions, problem-finding questions, dollar-quantifying questions, and the soft close that ends the call with "let me put something specific together for you."

You don't pitch on this call. You ask. They talk. They sell themselves.

## Why This Skill Exists

The discovery call is where most beginners blow the deal. They get nervous, start pitching their software, and the prospect mentally checks out. Or they ask vague questions, get vague answers, and end up with no actionable information.

This skill solves both problems by giving you the specific question stack that consistently surfaces:
1. A real, painful, expensive problem
2. Specific dollar figures
3. The objections you'll need to handle on the close call

## The Core Principle

**The prospect should be talking 70% of the time. You should be asking, listening, and taking notes.**

Your job on this call isn't to convince anyone of anything. It's to find out whether they have a problem worth solving, how much it costs them, and what they've already tried. If those three answers are good, you book a second call. If they aren't, you walk.

## How to Use This Skill

When the user activates this skill with an industry:

1. **Pull the painful problems** that industry typically faces (use Industry Download output if available).
2. **Map the questions to a 15-20 minute call structure**:
   - 2-3 minutes of rapport
   - 2 minutes of context (current state)
   - 5-8 minutes of problem digging
   - 3-5 minutes of dollar quantification
   - 2-3 minutes of soft close

3. **Output a tight script** the user can read off-screen during the call. Not 40 questions. The 8-12 best ones.

## Output Structure

### 1. The Call Frame (Verbatim Opener)
Exactly what to say in the first 60 seconds to establish:
- Why you're on the call
- What you'll do (ask questions)
- What you won't do (pitch them)
- That you're in control of the call

Example: *"I just want to ask you a few questions about your shop, see if I can help you. If it sounds like I can, I'll tell you exactly what I do. Sound good?"*

### 2. Rapport Questions (2-3 minutes)
Light, business-relevant warm-up. Not weather-talk. Get them comfortable answering questions.

- 2-3 specific questions about their business

### 3. Context Questions (2-3 minutes)
Establish the lay of the land. How big is the operation? What does a typical day look like?

- 3-4 questions that get them describing their current state

### 4. Problem-Finding Questions (5-8 minutes)
The core of the call. Funnel from broad to specific. Each question should narrow the focus toward the painful, expensive problem.

- Open with: a broad "what's working / not working" question
- Funnel down: 3-4 targeted questions about the specific area you suspect has the bleeding wound
- Get the admission: at least one question designed to make them say "yeah, this is a real problem"

For each question, include a **note on what to listen for** — the specific words that signal real pain vs. casual complaint.

### 5. Dollar-Quantifying Questions (3-5 minutes)
This is the closing nail. Get them to pull up their actual numbers.

- "How much is this costing you per month?"
- "What's your average [revenue per X / cost per Y]?"
- "How much are you spending on [their current bad solution]?"
- At least one question that forces them to do the math out loud

### 6. The Soft Close (2-3 minutes)
End the call with this exact pattern:

- Recap what they told you (shows you listened)
- Schedule the close call (don't ask "are you interested" — ask "when's a good time for the next call?")
- Tell them what you'll bring (a custom proposal with their numbers)

Verbatim closer template included.

### 7. Listen-For Cheat Sheet
A quick-reference list of:
- **Green flag phrases** (admissions of real pain) — "we lose about $X a month from this"
- **Yellow flag phrases** (worth probing further) — "we're not sure how to track that"
- **Red flag phrases** (this prospect won't close) — "we already have a solution that mostly works"

### 8. Common Objections to Watch For
3-5 objections likely to come up on this call, each with:
- The phrase to expect
- The reframe to deploy (without pitching)
- How to file it for the close call

## Pro Tips for Better Output

- **Tailor the language to the industry.** Auto shop owners say "ARO" and "decline work." Dentists say "treatment plans" and "case acceptance." Use their words.
- **Funnel, don't fish.** Each question should narrow the focus, not expand it. If you keep asking broader and broader questions, you've lost control of the call.
- **Always make them quantify.** If they say "this is a big problem," follow with "how big in dollars?" until you get a number.
- **Don't fix their objections on this call.** Note them, file them, fix them on the close call. The discovery call is for diagnosis only.

## When to Use This Skill

✅ Before any discovery call with a prospect
✅ When entering a new industry — to learn the question patterns
✅ When previous discovery calls haven't been surfacing the real problem

❌ Don't use as a sales script — these are diagnostic questions, not pitches
❌ Don't use after they've already committed to buy — different call, different script

## Remember

The prospect's own words are doing the selling. Your job is to ask the right questions and shut up.

**Ask. Listen. Quantify. Book the close.**
