# Client Locator

Find exactly where your buyers hang out online — and the exact words to send them when you get there.

## What This Skill Does

**Input:** A specific industry or niche (auto shops, HVAC contractors, dental offices, etc.)

**Output:** A targeted prospecting playbook with specific platforms (named groups, subreddits, forums), outreach scripts tailored to each platform, and a 7-day action plan to book your first 3 calls.

This skill solves the most common "I don't know where to start" problem. No more generic advice like "try LinkedIn." You get specific groups with member counts, real subreddits with subscriber numbers, and the exact opener for each platform.

## Why This Skill Exists

Most beginners waste their first two weeks looking for prospects in the wrong places. They try cold email, get 1% open rates, give up, and quit before they ever land a call.

The fastest path to your first client isn't email. It's showing up in the *exact place where business owners in your niche already gather* — and starting a conversation, not a pitch.

This skill tells you where those places are and how to talk once you get there.

## How to Use This Skill

When the user activates this skill with an industry:

1. **Web search aggressively.** Find specific Facebook groups, subreddits, forums, LinkedIn groups, Slack/Discord communities, and conferences for that exact industry. Include member counts and links where possible.
2. **Rank by reachability.** Some platforms have higher trust and reply rates than others. Surface the best ones first.
3. **Tailor outreach to each platform.** A Facebook DM script is different from a LinkedIn opener is different from a subreddit comment. Provide the right one for each.
4. **End with a 7-day action plan.** Specific daily actions that get the user from zero to 3 booked calls.

## Output Structure

### 1. The Top 5 Places to Find Them (Ranked)

For each location, provide:
- **Platform name and link**
- **Member/subscriber count**
- **Activity level** (dead, slow, active, very active)
- **Trust score** (1-10) — how warm is this audience to outreach?
- **Best move** — what to actually do here (post, DM, comment, lurk first)

Rank the top 5. Don't list 20 mediocre options. Concentrate firepower.

### 2. Facebook Groups (Specific Names + Member Counts)
- Group name 1 (member count) — link
- Group name 2 (member count) — link
- Group name 3 (member count) — link
- Etc.

### 3. Reddit Communities
- r/subreddit1 (subscriber count) — what they post about
- r/subreddit2 (subscriber count) — what they post about
- Etc.

### 4. Industry Forums
Specific named forums, with URLs, and a one-line description of who's there.

### 5. LinkedIn Groups
Specific LinkedIn groups with member counts.

### 6. Conferences and Events (Next 12 Months)
- Conference name, date, location
- Why this is a goldmine for in-person prospecting
- Estimated cost to attend

### 7. Outreach Scripts by Platform

Provide a tailored opener for each major platform. Each should be:
- 1-3 sentences max
- Question-driven (not pitch-driven)
- Sounds like a real person, not a template

**Facebook DM Opener:**
[Specific script using their language]

**Reddit Comment Strategy:**
[How to add value first, build credibility, then DM]

**LinkedIn Connection + Message:**
[Specific connection note + first message after they accept]

**In-Person at Conference:**
[Specific opener that doesn't feel salesy]

### 8. The 7-Day Action Plan

Day-by-day actions to go from zero to 3 booked calls:

- **Day 1:** Join 5 specific groups (named in the report). Read the rules. Lurk and watch.
- **Day 2:** Add 10 friends from those groups (specific filters to use).
- **Day 3:** Send 5 opener messages using the Facebook DM script.
- **Day 4:** Follow up on Day 3 messages. Send 5 more.
- **Day 5:** Comment on 3 high-engagement posts. Add value, no pitch.
- **Day 6:** DM the people whose posts you commented on. Reference the post.
- **Day 7:** Review what worked. Double down. Book your first calls.

### 9. Platform-Specific Pro Tips

Real tactical advice for each platform. Examples:
- "On Facebook, don't post about your software. Post a question. Engagement first, pitch later."
- "On Reddit, never DM cold. Comment helpfully on 5 posts before you reach out."
- "On LinkedIn, generic 'connect with me' notes get ignored. Reference their company specifically."

### 10. Red Flags to Avoid

- Groups that ban self-promotion (will get you kicked instantly)
- Subreddits that auto-remove posts from new accounts
- LinkedIn groups that are dead (under 10 posts a month)
- "Pay to play" forums where access requires industry credentials

## Pro Tips for Better Output

- **Be specific.** "Try Facebook" is useless. "Auto Shop Owners Hangout (47K members) — link" is gold.
- **Skip the dead zones.** Don't list groups with 200 members and no activity. Quality > quantity.
- **Match the platform's culture.** Facebook is relational. LinkedIn is professional. Reddit is informational. Outreach must match.
- **Always lead with curiosity, never with the pitch.** The opener's only job is to start a conversation. The pitch happens on the call.
